How To Build Apps With A-Frame

When it comes to building something quick in Altspace, it's hard to beat A-Frame. It has a simple HTML-based syntax, so anyone with even a passing familiarity with web development will pick it up in no time. If that's not you, you should go through MDN's terrific Introduction to HTML before you continue.

On the other hand, the world is full of web developers. If you're one of these, and feel comfortable experimenting without this tutorial holding your hand, you should jump right in with the A-Frame Documentation. You just need to add the Altspace component to your <a-scene> tag. Keep in mind though that development in Altspace has a few caveats.

What is A-Frame?

A-Frame is a relatively easy way to build a 3d scene without a lot of complicated coding. It's nearly as simple as adding an object, then telling it where it should be in the space. Take a look at the demo below:

This demo was constructed with a meer dozen lines of pseudo-HTML (plus some boilerplate): 

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html><body>
<script src="dist/aframe.min.js"></script>
<script src="dist/aframe-altspace-component.min.js"></script>

<a-scene altspace='vertical-align: bottom;'>
	<a-assets>
		<img id='moon' src='assets/moon.jpg'/>
		<img id='stars' src='assets/stars.png'/>
	</a-assets>
	<a-sphere position='0 1.5 0' material='shader: flat; src: #moon;'>
		<a-animation to='0 360 0' easing='linear' dur='20000' repeat='indefinite'/>
	</a-sphere>
	<a-sky src='#stars' radius='2500'></a-sky>
	<a-entity position='0 0 2'>
		<a-camera></a-camera>
	</a-entity>
</a-scene>

</body></html>
